# Clip Creation Rules — get it right on the FIRST cut (so there are no revisions)

> Locked 2026-06-11. These are recurring revision notes turned into CREATION-time rules. Apply them at
> MINE/PICK/CUT (spine steps 3–6) BEFORE rendering — the goal is to one-shot a podcast clip and never
> hand it back for the same fix. Operator: "I'm tired of doing your revisions — bake them into the skills
> that created these so it doesn't make it again." Brand-agnostic: rules operate on the speaker's own
> words; never invent lines.

## The hard rules (each one is a revision we already paid for — DO NOT repeat)

1. **LEAD WITH THE PAYOFF — never bury the hook.** The most relatable / surprising / quotable line is
   the FIRST thing the clip says. If the gold sits in the middle or end of the answer, **reorder** so it
   opens the clip. Weak openers that got flagged: continuation words ("I also see…", "And so…"), an
   abstract thesis, or jargon. (Fix proven: "people are very surprised by their lives" moved from 0:40
   to the hook; "what used to feel good doesn't feel as good after a hundred times" replaced the jargon
   lead.)

2. **COLD-VIEWER CONTEXT (non-negotiable).** Read the script as someone with ZERO prior context. Every
   line must make sense alone. No dangling reference — a "that / this / also / he" pointing at something
   you cut. A Q&A or reaction clip MUST carry the question or the setup it answers. If a line only makes
   sense because of something off-screen, add the one setup line that grounds it or cut it. (Adding "I
   have high standards — that's my job, to hold the standard" grounded the dangling "I *also* see…".)

3. **THE BRAND PERSON CARRIES THE CLIP.** On the brand's channel, the brand person must be the primary voice.
   If the strongest line is the GUEST/interviewer's, either lead with the **brand person's own** take on that
   theme (search the WHOLE transcript for where they say it) or don't cut the clip. A clip where the
   brand person barely or never speaks is not a clip for their channel. (A guest-only "potential vs
   pattern" cut got flagged: "we're making clips for Speaker and Speaker isn't even talking.")

4. **FINISH THE POINT — don't cut the arc short.** Include the complete thought/story to its real payoff.
   If the speaker is mid-build, keep going until they land it; never end on a half-made point. (Burnout
   "ends way too early — let him finish his point.")

5. **EARN THE LENGTH WITH SUBSTANCE.** Don't ship a thin 10–16s clip when the moment supports more. Build
   it: setup up front + the full point + the continuation/solution after. Match the moment's natural
   length, don't truncate a strong moment to keep it short.

6. **END ON THE BRAND PERSON — check the LAST frame.** The final frame must be the brand person, NEVER the
   interviewer/guest (a hanging interviewer frame or reaction cutaway at the very end is an instant flag).
   If the cut lands on the wrong face, trim the last 1–2 frames. (Verify the last frame at QC, every clip.)

7. **NO LEADING FLUFF.** Start exactly on the hook word — trim any "so / um / yeah" or breath before it
   (this is the head-trim gate, spine 7d). The first sound is the hook.

8. **KILL FILLER ON THE FIRST PASS.** Remove "um/uh", false-starts, and restarts in the FIRST cut — never
   leave them for a revision. Scan the cut transcript for them before reframing. (Repeated "cut an um at
   0:20 / 0:27" notes.)

## How to apply (at PICK time, step 4)

For every candidate, before you write `cuts.json`:
1. Draft the script **as it will play**: hook (the payoff line) → context/setup → build → hard payoff.
2. Cold-viewer-read it (rule 2). Confirm the **brand person carries it** (rule 3) and it **ends on them**
   (rule 6).
3. Confirm it **finishes the point** (rule 4) and has **enough substance** (rule 5) — pull adjacent or
   cross-pod material if thin.
4. Map to master word-index spans; cut with filler removed (rule 8) and no leading fluff (rule 7).
5. VERIFY by transcript (no clipped/leaked words, hook lands first, payoff lands last), then cold-viewer-
   read once more. Only then reframe.

These are the same few moves every time — **lead with the payoff, ground the context, keep the brand
person on screen start-to-finish, finish the point, no filler.** Do them at creation; don't wait for a note.
