# Speaker Q&A Short-Form Editing Structure (full SOP)

Source: internal Speaker Q&A SOP doc (2026). Applies to two-person interactive Q&A content. Keep this document verbatim — it's the canonical brief.

## Picking the moment

A great Q&A moment has:
1. Clear introduction
2. Clear problem
3. Tension
4. Widely applicable
5. Clear payoff

**Questions to ask yourself:**
- Is this too niche?
- Is the problem clear?
- Is there clear tension?

## Structure — who, what, how

### Hook

**Hook visual:**
- Text hook title card
- Split-screen with Speaker on top → switch to Speaker full-screen or guest full-screen
- OR: Speaker/Guest full-screen → switch to Speaker/Guest full-screen

**Hook audible:** "We make Y, our business is X" / "Our business is X, we make Y"
- **Don't include** "Hi, my name is…" or Speaker saying "Hey man what's up, what can I help you with?" — unless necessary
- **Start right at the meat.** "I sell X, and I don't know how to Y even though I've tried for 3 years…"

**Good Hook Filter** (has at least one of the two):
1. **Short preface** before the interaction (≤15s) — e.g. dentist example (12s), time study example (13s), no-sales example (11s)
2. **Attention-grabber** in the hook — e.g. the `$10M` example

### Problem-solving / Tension

This is where we establish context around the problem, leading up to the solution. Ideally also where Speaker confronts the point of tension.

1. **Starts with:**
   - Speaker prompts a question to get context on the business owner's issue
   - The guest says something that prompts a reaction from Speaker
2. **Then:**
   - Speaker digs into the issue, gaining *necessary* context, lays the foundation for the solution
   - The guest provides brief bits of info or reactions
3. **Goal:** only enough necessary context for the solution to make sense
4. **We solve ONE issue, not multiple.**

**Questions to ask yourself:**
- What are we trying to solve?
- How can we provide the most value in the shortest amount of time?
- Did I include any unnecessary context (caveats, side quests)?
- What would apply to most other businesses as well?

### Solution / Payoff

Objective: provide **one** solution, not multiple.

We've established the issue, established tension, extracted necessary context, confronted the issue, and now we provide one viable solution.

**Solution can be:**
- Actionable advice
- Belief-breaker
- Speaker's professional opinion

**Good filter for a solution:**
- Widely applicable (answers a question many business owners have, not just the one Speaker is talking to)
- Objectively easy to understand
- Clearly related to the established problem

**Questions to ask yourself:**
- Did we solve the issue? (Sometimes there are multiple issues — pick the one that provides the most audience value.)
- Does this provide value to our audience?

## Brand Alignment

Q&A's involve creative decisions that alter the original interaction. Always ask:

1. **Did I protect the overall authenticity of the moment?**
   - Does this make Speaker look good?
   - Is Speaker's message represented accurately?
   - Is the guest represented accurately?
   - Did I restructure any moments out of context that misrepresent Speaker or the guest?
2. **Is Speaker's advice represented the way it was intended?** The restructuring must be *parallel* to what Speaker is saying, not *perpendicular*.
3. How could this be **bad** for the brand?
4. How could this be **good** for the brand?
5. Does this increase or decrease the likelihood that more people respect Speaker as an authority on business?
6. Does this increase or decrease the likelihood that more people want to attend the workshops or do business with Brand?

## Pro tips (editor workflow)

- **Color-code cuts** by:
  - Stage 1: segment (hook, profit, AI, growth rate, money, tension…)
  - Stage 2: camera angle (Speaker, guest, wide)
  - Do both — first segment, then angles
- **Write out the individual segments and potential structures** before cutting
- **Experiment** — do multiple takes on the structure. Avoid sunk-cost bias of "it has to be this way". Duplicate the draft and alter the duplicate
- **Kill your darlings.** Solving one specific issue is the goal. An amazing moment that doesn't fit the structure: either rework the structure extensively to include it, or cut it.
