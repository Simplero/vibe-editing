# Hook Framework — What Makes a Podcast Clip Go Viral

Load this file when scoring the first 3 seconds of a candidate clip.

## The 8 hook archetypes

### 1. Contrarian (weight: high)
**Pattern:** Contradicts a commonly-held belief in the first sentence.

Trigger phrases:
- "Most people think..."
- "Everyone says X, but actually..."
- "The conventional wisdom is wrong..."
- "You've been told X your whole life. It's false."
- "X is actually the worst thing you can do."

Example openers:
- "Most founders think product-market fit is a moment. It's not."
- "Going to college is the worst financial decision you can make."

Score 0.9+ if the contrarian claim is specific and names the thing being contradicted.

---

### 2. Story (weight: high)
**Pattern:** Drops the viewer into a specific moment with stakes.

Trigger phrases:
- "So this one time..."
- "I'll never forget the day..."
- "A few years ago I was..."
- "Three weeks into the project..."
- "I get this call from..."

Score 0.9+ if the opener names a specific time, place, or person. Score lower if it's a generic "once upon a time."

---

### 3. Stat (weight: high)
**Pattern:** Leads with a surprising specific number.

Trigger phrases:
- "97% of..."
- "Only 3 out of 100..."
- "$4.2 million dollars..."
- "In the last 18 months..."

Score 0.9+ if the number is specific (not rounded), surprising, and directly relevant to the topic.

---

### 4. Confession (weight: medium-high)
**Pattern:** Shares something personal / vulnerable / previously hidden.

Trigger phrases:
- "I've never told anyone this..."
- "Here's something I'm not proud of..."
- "The truth is I was..."
- "For years I hid the fact that..."

Score high only when the confession is specific and stakes are clear.

---

### 5. Stakes (weight: medium-high)
**Pattern:** Names a concrete bad outcome the viewer will experience.

Trigger phrases:
- "If you don't do X, you'll..."
- "The biggest mistake that kills..."
- "This one thing will tank your..."
- "You're one decision away from..."

Score based on how specific the negative consequence is.

---

### 6. Question (weight: medium)
**Pattern:** Asks a question the viewer genuinely wants answered.

Trigger phrases:
- "Why do 90% of..."
- "What's the difference between..."
- "Have you ever wondered..."
- "Why does nobody talk about..."

IMPORTANT: the clip MUST answer the question before the end. Open-loop hooks without payoff score low.

---

### 7. List (weight: medium)
**Pattern:** Promises enumerated value.

Trigger phrases:
- "Three reasons why..."
- "The five biggest mistakes..."
- "Here are four things..."

The clip must actually deliver all items in the list. Partial lists score low.

---

### 8. Reveal (weight: medium)
**Pattern:** Promises insider knowledge.

Trigger phrases:
- "Here's what nobody tells you..."
- "The secret to X is..."
- "What they don't teach you in..."
- "Behind the scenes, what actually happens..."

Score high only if the reveal is specific and substantive, not vague.

---

## Disqualifiers (automatic reject)

A candidate is rejected if its first 3 seconds:
- Start mid-sentence
- Reference something earlier ("like I said before", "as we discussed")
- Contain a generic filler opener ("um so yeah basically")
- Reference the interviewer's question without context ("yeah that's a great point")
- Are just agreement ("right, right, exactly")

## Ending scoring

The last 5 seconds must:
- Land on a complete sentence
- Contain a takeaway, punchline, or pivot
- NOT trail into "and another thing..." or "which reminds me of..."

Strong endings:
- A one-liner conclusion: "That's why I never hire from Ivy League schools."
- A rhetorical question that makes the viewer think
- A concrete call-to-action framing: "So the next time you're in that situation, remember this."

## Self-containment check

Reject clips that contain:
- Pronouns without antecedents ("he said that..." — who?)
- References to prior stories ("like that founder I mentioned")
- Mid-conversation context ("right, and then after that call...")

If the viewer needs to have watched the previous 5 minutes to understand the clip, it's not shortable.
