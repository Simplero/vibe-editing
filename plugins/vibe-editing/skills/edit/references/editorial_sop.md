# Speaker Q&A SF Editing SOP (distilled)

Full source: user-provided Notion doc "Speaker Q&A SF Editing Structure" + "Trevor Bible".

## Picking the moment

Good Q&A moment characteristics:
- **Clear introduction** — setup of who/what.
- **Clear problem** — viewer knows what's being solved.
- **Tension** — why it matters, what's at stake.
- **Widely applicable** — works for more than one niche.
- **Clear payoff** — actionable advice, belief-breaker, or Speaker's professional opinion.

Ask yourself per candidate:
- Is this too niche?
- Is the problem clear?
- Is there clear tension?
- Does this provide value to a cold audience with zero context on the brand?

## Structure: Hook → Problem/Tension → Solution/Payoff

### Hook (verbal, visual, text — think all three)
- **Verbal**: 5 words or less. Start mid-sentence. Skip "hi my name is…" and "hey man what's up." Jump straight to the meat.
- **Visual**: splitscreen OR full-face of speaker. No wide establishing.
- **Text**: title card should give immediate context.
- Good hook filters (need at least one):
  1. Short preface (≤15s) before the interaction, OR
  2. Attention-grabber line in the first 3s.

### Problem / Tension
- Establish context AROUND the problem.
- Only enough context for the solution to make sense. **No side quests.**
- One problem per clip. Not multiple.

### Solution / Payoff
- **One** solution per clip. Not a list.
- Widely applicable, objectively easy to understand.
- Keep the takeaway statement for the **very end** ("one big thing we've noticed on top performers").

## Pacing and structure rules (Trevor Bible)

- 30–40 seconds is the sweet spot. 15–30s can work; 2min+ is long for SF.
- Add **3 extra video frames** at start (30 = 30fps, 6 = 60fps) so the clip doesn't land mid-syllable.
- **S-curve audio fade** on first 2 and last 2 frames of every short (avoids clicks).
- Leave 1–2 frames before start so it doesn't begin mid-sentence.
- Structure is #1 priority. VFX is secondary.
- Cut every start/end of clips, then refine.
- Kill your darlings — a great moment that doesn't fit the structure gets cut.
- Ending "unexpectedly" increases rewatchability (looping, twist, tight sweet-message end).
- Story arc: think in "THEREFORE / BUT" not "AND THEN".

## Speaker's filler words (cut these)

Single-token: `um`, `uh`, `uhm`, `umm`, `uhh`.

Phrases (remove whole phrase):
- "the thing is is that"
- "and so for context"
- "so if you think about"
- "for me, personally…"
- "for example"

Softer fillers (cut when they precede a scroll-cue vibe, keep when mid-flow):
- "now…"
- "and so…"
- "fundamentally…"
- "like…"
- "right?…"

Trevor's note: these phrases signal "lesson coming up" and can trigger a scroll. Cutting them keeps pace tight.

## Brand alignment check

Before shipping a cut, ask:
- Did I protect the authenticity of the moment?
- Is Speaker's advice represented the way it was intended?
- Would this make Speaker look good to a cold audience who doesn't know the brand?
- Does this increase respect for Speaker as a business authority?

## Notes that shape the cuts

- **No relationship content** from Speaker (that's Creator's lane).
- **No mention of other platforms** ("like what I said on TikTok" → kills YouTube performance and vice versa).
- **No "Modern Wisdom" clips get color-graded** (Chris Williamson's team is meticulous about their palette).
- **Lower-thirds are banned on TikTok** (flagged as watermarked).
- **Censor + bleep curse words** in first 15s (YT policy).

## Hook refinement (visual / verbal / text)

The strongest shorts load attention-grabbing words EARLY. For listicles, don't list in spoken order — reorder so "money" beats "leverage" in the hook position.
