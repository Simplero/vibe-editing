# /highlight — title rules (subscriber-optimized)

**PRIMARY KPI = `subs_per_1k_views` (subscriber conversion). NOT CTR.**
The job of a highlights channel is to grow subscribers, so the job of the title is to pull the
*right* viewer who then subscribes — not to win the click. Re-derive the ranking on your own
YouTube Studio export (`highlight_studio_import.py`); the patterns below are the method.

---

## The core finding: CTR and subscriber-conversion are DECOUPLED

The highest-CTR title styles tend to be the **worst** subscriber converters:

| | CTR | follows/1k |
|---|---|---|
| "Why…" / curiosity / reaction-personality | **highest** | **lowest** |
| "You/Your" declarative, quoted question, contrarian | lower | **highest** |

**Rule: chase the subscribe, not the click.** Only reach for a curiosity/"Why" title when the
explicit goal of that upload is raw reach.

---

## Pattern ranking by subs_per_1k_views (lead with the top patterns)

1. **"Watch this if you're [specific struggle]"** — top converter *(small n, but standout)*
   e.g. *"Watch this if you're working hard, but not making progress"*
2. **"You / Your [declarative truth or command]"**
   e.g. *"You need to upsell."*, *"You're Stuck Because You Can't Keep Customers"*, *"You Need Recurring Revenue"*
3. **Quoted business question** (the Q&A/hotline signature)
   e.g. *"Should I start a new business or keep my current one?"*, *"I grow sales teams, how do I scale?"*
4. **"Helping a [chunked business] [specific tension/outcome]"**
   e.g. *"Helping a Lead-Gen Company Create a Reverse Affiliate Offer"*
5. **"How to [specific outcome]"**
6. **Number-lead** ("1 Habit That Fixes 90% of Problems") — big reach, medium subs
- ⚠️ **"Why…"** — best CTR, **worst subs**. Reach play only.

Hook-type signal agrees: **contrarian + question convert; curiosity + story don't.**

---

## Format defaults

- **Q&A / hotline** (a caller + host): default to **(a)** the caller's quoted question *if it's one a viewer would also ask*, or **(b)** `Helping a [business chunked up one level] [the specific tension]`.
  - **Chunk the niche up one level:** Glamping Resort → Resort Business; Immigration Law Firm → Law Firm; Dirt Bike Coach → Coaching Business.
  - Name the **tension**, not just "scale": *"…Quit"*, *"…Charge What They're Worth"*, *"…Get to $50M/Year"*.
- **Monologue / podcast clip** (host teaching): `You/Your` declarative or `How to [outcome]`.

## Hard rules

- Optimize for subscribers; a curiosity/"Why" title is allowed ONLY when the goal is reach.
- **Steal the moment** — use the strongest *verbatim* line; pull the real number from THIS transcript.
- ≤ ~70 characters. One idea. **Fully self-contained** (a stranger gets it cold — no context bleed from another video).
- **NEVER** mention mom / mother / family. No vague teasers ("…for a reason", "…you won't believe"). No em-dashes (use periods). No formula labels ("HELPING", "WHY") in the final string.
- Every title must trace to a specific line in THIS transcript.
- **Quoted-question titles MUST keep the quotation marks** — `"Should I start a new business or keep my current one?"`. The quotes are the proven style *and* how the miner recognizes the pattern; drop them and it reads as a generic statement and scores lower.

## A/B/C angles (for the 3-title scheduler) — re-ranked for subs

- **A — sub play:** "You/…" declarative OR the quoted question (highest f1k).
- **B — specific-viewer pain:** "You're [struggling with X]" / "Watch this if you're [struggle]".
- **C — reach play:** contrarian bold claim OR a real surprising number (the curiosity slot — flagged as the riskiest for subs, never a vague tease).
